Why This Segmentation

Academic retina centers are the highest priority not because they generate the most volume, but because they generate the most valuable volume. A published study from Johns Hopkins, Bascom Palmer, or Wills Eye carries more weight with payers and physicians than 10,000 tests at a community practice.

Large retina practices are the volume engine of Year 1. These are groups of 5+ retina specialists seeing high volumes of AMD patients daily. They already manage AMD patients and understand the clinical need for earlier detection.

Primary care networks are included as early pilots to test the primary care distribution thesis before committing significant sales resources in Year 2.

Why Founder-Led Sales Matters

In diagnostic company early stages, the founder is the most effective sales person. Not because they are trained in sales, but because physicians evaluating a novel diagnostic want to speak to the scientist who developed it.

Exact Sciences' Kevin Conroy personally visited oncologists during Cologuard's early launch. Foundation Medicine's founders presented directly to cancer centers. This pattern repeats across successful diagnostic launches.

Why 7.4% Physician Penetration in Year 5 Is Conservative

For context, comparable diagnostic tests at maturity:

  • Oncotype DX: ordered by approximately 80% of eligible oncologists
  • Cologuard: ordered by approximately 35% of primary care physicians
  • Foundation Medicine: ordered by approximately 60% of oncologists

A1 Diagnosis at 7.4% overall penetration (60% of retina specialists, 31% of ophthalmologists, 5% of primary care) is well below mature diagnostic adoption rates. This leaves substantial upside beyond Year 5.

Tests Per Physician: Reasonableness Check

Is 64-73 tests per physician per year realistic?

Benchmark Tests/Physician/Year Context
Cholesterol screening 300-500 Routine preventive care
PSA screening 150-250 Targeted preventive care
Cologuard orders 100-200 Screening-age patients
Oncotype DX 30-60 Narrow indication
A1 Diagnosis target 64-73 Risk assessment for 55+ patients

At 64 tests per physician per year, that is approximately 1.2 tests per week. For a retina specialist seeing 30-40 patients per day, ordering 1-2 blood-based AMD risk tests per week is modest. For a primary care physician, 45 tests per year is less than 1 per week.

Conservative Assumption

If AMD blood testing becomes part of routine preventive care (like cholesterol), per-physician volumes could be 5-10x higher than our projections.

5. Operational Scaling Plan

Lab Capacity Model

Core Constraint: LC-MS/MS Throughput

The LC-MS/MS instrument is the rate-limiting factor for test volume. Each instrument has maximum daily throughput depending on run time per sample and operating hours.

  • Run time per sample: 15-20 minutes
  • Samples per batch: 10-12
  • Batches per instrument per day (single shift): 4-5
  • Tests per instrument per day (single shift): 40-60
  • Tests per instrument per day (double shift): 80-120

Key Insight for Investors

Diagnostic tests have among the highest margins in healthcare. The cost of running a blood test does not increase proportionally with volume. Lab reagent costs are approximately $15-25 per test regardless of volume, and fixed costs (facility, equipment, personnel) are spread across increasing volumes.

This creates dramatic margin expansion as scale increases â€” from 44% EBITDA margin in Year 1 to 83% by Year 5.
