The Critical Problem

Current detection methods identify the disease only after irreversible damage has occurred, when treatment options are limited and costly.

! Late Detection = Lost Vision

  • Currently, patients must visit specialized retina specialists or ophthalmologists to detect AMD
  • As a result, most Americans only seek eye care after vision loss has already begun

Critical Challenge: Progressive and irreversible vision loss occurs over time. When vision problems become apparent, the damage is permanent and irreversible.

AMD Progression Stages:

  • Early Stage (mild central vision changes)
  • Intermediate Stage (increasing central vision loss)
  • Advanced Dry AMD (significant central vision loss)
  • Advanced Wet AMD (severe central vision loss)
