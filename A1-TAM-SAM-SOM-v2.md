# A1 Diagnosis — TAM, SAM, SOM Market Sizing (v2)

> **Purpose:** Single source of truth for pitch decks, investor materials, and website content. All SOM figures are anchored to the bottom-up 5-year financial model. All corrections from cross-model review (March 2026) are incorporated.

## Context & Product Overview

- **Product:** First blood-based AMD (Age-related Macular Degeneration) risk assessment platform
- **Technology:** LC-MS/MS native peptide analysis + AI/ML risk scoring (94-95% accuracy)
- **Regulatory status:** CLIA-certified LDT — no FDA clearance required to commercialize
- **Launch target:** Q1 2027
- **Price:** $250/test (B2B invoice to optometry/ophthalmology clinics)
- **Channel:** B2B only — optometry and ophthalmology practices (NOT direct-to-consumer, NOT primary care)
- **Language discipline:** Always "risk assessment" — never "diagnostic," "screening," or "detection" without qualifier

## TAM — Total Addressable Market: $42.3 Billion

| Component | Figure | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Americans aged 35+ without AMD diagnosis | 169.1 million | U.S. Census Bureau Vintage 2024 (NC-EST2024-AGESEX) |
| Test price | $250 | A1 Diagnosis pricing model |
| **TAM** | **$42.3B** | **169.1M x $250** |

### Key Points
- 20 million Americans currently have AMD (excluded from TAM — already diagnosed)
- ~4.5 million Americans turn 35 each year, continuously expanding the addressable population
- TAM represents the prevention/risk assessment market, distinct from the $10-18B AMD treatment market

### Why 35+ (Not 50+)
Clinical demand exists below age 50. Retina specialists report patients as young as 25 seeking AMD risk information after a family member's diagnosis. The test addresses anyone in the eye care ecosystem with a reason to assess AMD risk — not just those already at elevated clinical risk.

## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market: $7.4 Billion

### Definition
The portion of TAM reachable through A1's exclusive B2B channel (optometry/ophthalmology clinics), filtered for patients with clinical candidacy for AMD risk assessment.

### Filtering Methodology

| Step | Filter | Remaining Population | Source |
|------|--------|---------------------|--------|
| Start | Americans 35+ without AMD | 169.1M | U.S. Census Bureau 2024 |
| Filter 1 | Annual eye care visit (~50% of 35+) | ~84.5M | NHIS eye care utilization data (see note below) |
| Filter 2 | AMD risk factors (≥1 risk factor, ~35%) | **~29.6M** | CDC VEHSS, JAMA Ophthalmology 2022, AHA 2024 |

**SAM = 29.6M x $250 = ~$7.4 Billion**

### Filter 1: Eye Care Visit Rate — Source Detail
- **All adults 18+:** 43.1% visited an eye care professional in the past 12 months (NHIS 2017, JAMA Ophthalmology 2020)
- **Adults at high risk for vision loss:** 56.9% visited an eye doctor in the past year
- **Adults 35+:** ~50% (interpolation between 43.1% all-adult rate and 56.9% high-risk rate; higher utilization due to age-related vision needs)
- **Honest characterization:** This is an interpolation, not a direct statistic. If pressed by investors, acknowledge the estimate. Even at 40% instead of 50%, SAM yields ~$5.9B — same order of magnitude.

### Filter 2: AMD Risk Stratification
The ~35% filter captures overlapping risk categories (deduplicated):

| Risk Factor | Prevalence | Source |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Adults 50+ (highest baseline AMD risk) | ~117M of the 169M pool | U.S. Census Bureau 2024 |
| Current smokers (2-3.5x increased risk) | 10.1% of adults | CDC BRFSS 2024 |
| Family history of AMD (first-degree relative) | 15-20% | JAMA Ophthalmology |
| Cardiovascular comorbidity (shared pathophysiology) | ~48% of adults 50+ | AHA Heart Disease & Stroke Statistics 2024 |
| Family-driven proactive seekers (under 50) | Unmeasured but clinically observed | Retina specialist reports |

**After removing overlaps:** ~29.6M unique individuals with ≥1 clinical risk factor or documented family-driven motivation.

### Why These Two Filters (Not Others)
- **Eye care visit rate** is the correct primary filter because A1's channel is exclusively optometry/ophthalmology. Patients who don't visit eye doctors are unreachable through the B2B model regardless of risk status.
- **AMD risk factors** reflect clinical likelihood of test being ordered. Eye care providers order risk assessment for patients with identifiable risk factors, not universally.
- **Insurance/general healthcare engagement** filters were deliberately excluded — they apply to primary care channels, not eye care specifically, and would overstate the reachable market.

### Alternative Methodology Note: Broader SAM ($12.5B)
An alternative approach using general healthcare engagement filters (91.8% insured → 85.2% physician visit rate → 38% risk factors) yields ~50M eligible individuals and $12.5B SAM. This broader figure may apply if A1 expands beyond eye care into primary care or DTC channels. **For current B2B eye care channel, $7.4B is the defensible figure.**

If an investor challenges $7.4B as conservative, the response is: *"Yes — we excluded the primary care channel, the under-35 family-driven segment, employer wellness programs, and DTC entirely. Those are all upside."*

### Upside Segment (Not in Core SAM)
Retina specialists report demand-pull from patients under 35 with first-degree AMD relatives actively seeking risk assessment. This family-history-driven under-35 cohort is a measurable expansion segment not captured in the current SAM. Flag as upside, not core.

## SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market: $1.0M → $100M (Year 1 → Year 5)

### Definition
Revenue A1 Diagnosis projects to capture, built bottom-up from clinic partnerships x tests per clinic x test price.

### CRITICAL: SOM = Bottom-Up Financial Model ONLY
SOM is NOT calculated top-down from SAM percentage. It is derived from the operational 5-year growth model and then expressed as SAM/TAM penetration for context. Any top-down SOM figures (e.g., "$62.5M = 0.5% x SAM") must be deleted from all materials — they are mathematically inconsistent with the operational model.

### 5-Year SOM Trajectory

| Metric | Y1 (2027) | Y2 (2028) | Y3 (2029) | Y4 (2030) | Y5 (2031) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Phase** | CLIA LDT Launch | CLIA LDT Scale | De Novo Submission | FDA Clearance + Insurance | National Scale |
| **Partner Clinics** | 15 → 30 | 75 | 150 → 300 | 600 | 1,200 |
| **Tests/Month (exit)** | 450 | 1,125 | 6,600 | 15,000 | 33,000 |
| **Annual Tests** | 4,050 | 13,500 | 55,800 | 180,000 | 400,000 |
| **Revenue (SOM)** | **$1.0M** | **$3.4M** | **$14.0M** | **$45.0M** | **$100M** |
| **% of SAM ($7.4B)** | 0.013% | 0.046% | 0.189% | 0.608% | 1.35% |
| **% of TAM ($42.3B)** | 0.002% | 0.008% | 0.033% | 0.107% | 0.237% |
| **Team Size** | 5 | 12 | 25 | 40 | 60 |
| **Key Milestone** | CLIA LDT + PLA code application | PLA code active + payer negotiations | De Novo submission filed | FDA clearance + CMS reimbursement | National coverage |
| **Cumulative Tests** | 4,050 | 17,550 | 73,350 | 253,350 | 653,350 |

### Bottom-Up Build Logic

**Year 1 (2027) — CLIA LDT Launch:**
- Start with 15 clinics, grow to 30 by year-end
- ~15 tests/clinic/month at year-end (450 tests/month)
- Annual: 4,050 tests x $250 = $1.0M
- Self-pay + HSA/FSA only
- PLA code application submitted immediately at launch

**Year 2 (2028) — CLIA LDT Expansion:**
- 75 partner clinics
- ~15 tests/clinic/month (1,125 tests/month)
- Annual: 13,500 tests x $250 = $3.4M
- PLA code active (~Q4 2027/Q1 2028) — used for payer negotiations
- Private payer contract discussions begin

**Year 3 (2029) — De Novo Submission:**
- Clinics grow from 150 → 300 as market awareness builds
- ~22 tests/clinic/month at year-end (6,600 tests/month)
- Annual: 55,800 tests x $250 = $14.0M
- Clinical validation study complete; De Novo + Breakthrough Device Designation submitted
- 4.1x revenue jump driven by growing clinical evidence + expanding clinic network

**Year 4 (2030) — FDA Clearance + Insurance:**
- 600 partner clinics
- ~25 tests/clinic/month (15,000 tests/month)
- Annual: 180,000 tests x $250 = $45.0M
- FDA De Novo clearance (~312 days review with Breakthrough Designation)
- CMS reimbursement removes patient payment barrier
- 3.2x revenue jump driven by institutional adoption + insurance coverage

**Year 5 (2031) — National Scale:**
- 1,200 partner clinics
- ~27.5 tests/clinic/month (33,000 tests/month)
- Annual: 400,000 tests x $250 = $100M
- National distribution, full insurance coverage
- Still only 1.35% of SAM — massive upside remains

### Why 1.35% SAM Penetration at Year 5 Is Conservative

1. **Simple blood draw** = faster adoption curve than imaging-based tests or stool-based tests (Cologuard)
2. **No competition** in blood-based AMD risk assessment — first-mover in uncontested space
3. **Insurance removes friction** starting Y4 — dramatically expands eligible patient pool
4. **1,200 clinics is modest** — there are ~44,000 optometry practices and ~18,000 ophthalmology practices in the U.S. (less than 2% penetration)
5. **At IPO, diagnostic companies typically capture 0.1-2% of TAM** — A1 at 0.237% TAM / 1.35% SAM is well within precedent
6. **Industry precedent:** Exact Sciences reached ~$2.76B revenue representing meaningful penetration of colorectal cancer screening TAM

### Phase Transition Catalysts
- **CLIA → FDA:** De Novo clearance unlocks hospital systems, academic medical centers, and large optometry chains that require FDA-cleared products
- **FDA → Insurance:** CMS reimbursement code eliminates $250 out-of-pocket barrier, converting "interested but won't pay" patients into tested patients
- **Insurance → National Scale:** Payer coverage enables national distribution partnerships (e.g., Quest Diagnostics, Labcorp patient access sites)

## Unit Economics

### Per-Test Economics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Revenue per test | $250 |
| Variable COGS | $60/test |
| **Contribution Margin** | **$190/test (76%)** |
| Monthly Fixed Costs | $34,700/month |
| **Breakeven** | **~183 tests/month (~9 tests/business day)** |

### Variable COGS Breakdown (per test)

| Cost Component | Amount | Detail |
|----------------|--------|--------|
| Reagents & consumables | $26 | Solvents, buffers, LC column wear, calibrants |
| Sample collection kit | $7 | Blood tubes, labels, packaging |
| Shipping (cold chain) | $23 | Temperature-controlled transport |
| AI/ML compute | $3 | Cloud processing per sample |
| Report generation | $1 | Risk report creation & delivery |
| **Total Variable COGS** | **$60** | |

**Native peptide advantage:** Skipping the trypsin digestion step saves $10-15/test vs traditional proteomics.

### Monthly Fixed Costs

| Category | Amount | Components |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Production Fixed (COGS) | $23,200/mo | LC-MS/MS lease $15K + CLS tech $3.2K + QC $5K |
| SG&A Fixed Costs | $11,500/mo | Lab director $5K + Lab space $4K + LIS/IT $2.5K |
| **Total Fixed** | **$34,700/mo** | |

### Gross Margin at Scale (500 tests/month)
- Fixed production COGS per test: $23,200 / 500 = $46/test
- Gross Margin: $250 - $60 - $46 = **$144/test (58%)**
- SG&A sits below the gross margin line

|---------|-------------|---------------|

| Exact Sciences | 69% | $2.76B |
| Guardant Health | 62% | $622M |

### Reimbursement Pathway (Corrected with PLA Codes)

| Milestone | Timing | Activity |
|-----------|--------|----------|

### Why PLA Codes (Not Category I CPT)

| | PLA Code | Category I CPT |
|--|----------|----------------|
| Timeline | 6-9 months | 3-5 years |
| Requirement | Sole-source laboratory test | Broad clinical usage data |
| A1 qualifies? | Yes — single lab, proprietary test | Not until years of clinical data |
| Precedent | Exact Sciences, GRAIL, BillionToOne | Long-term play only |
| Billing priority | Takes precedence over unlisted codes | N/A |
| Reimbursement guarantee? | No — PLA code enables billing but doesn't guarantee payer acceptance | N/A |

**Corrected timeline:** PLA code replaces "miscellaneous CPT codes" in Y1. Category I CPT remains a longer-term play alongside FDA submission.

### FDA De Novo Timeline (Corrected)

| Stage | Timing | Activity |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Y1-Y2 (2027-2028) | CLIA LDT revenue | Clinical validation study enrollment |
| Mid-Y3 (mid-2029) | Clinical study complete | Breakthrough Device Designation submitted |

### IDx-DR Precedent (Closest Structural Analog)
- Filed De Novo: February 5, 2018
- FDA clearance: April 11, 2018 (~65 days — outlier speed)
- Had Breakthrough Device Designation
- First autonomous AI diagnostic in ophthalmology
- Average De Novo with Breakthrough: ~312 days (2019-2023 data)
- **Takeaway:** Y4 clearance is fully supported. IDx-DR proves ophthalmology AI + De Novo + Breakthrough can clear in under 12 months from submission.

## B2B Service Flow

```
Eye Doctor Orders → Blood Draw at Clinic → A1 CLIA Lab Analyzes → Risk Report to Doctor → Clinic Pays A1 $250
```

### Money Flow (Three-Party Model)

| Party | Role | Economics |
|-------|------|----------|
| **A1 Diagnosis** | Invoices clinic $250/test | Lab services model — one clean B2B relationship |
| **Eye Doctor / Clinic** | Orders test, bills patient insurance | Keeps reimbursement margin — revenue-positive service |
| **Patient** | Gets test at routine eye visit | No extra visit needed — accessible through existing appointment |

## Key Investor Talking Points

1. **"Our Year 5 target of $100M represents 1.35% of our serviceable market."**
   Demonstrates massive upside runway while keeping projections grounded.

2. **"Every number in our SOM is built bottom-up from clinic count x tests per clinic x price."**
   No top-down assumptions. Investors can challenge any individual assumption.

3. **"FDA clearance in Year 4 is the growth accelerator — but the business works without it."**
   CLIA LDT generates revenue from Y1. FDA is a catalyst, not a prerequisite.

4. **"At 1,200 clinics in Year 5, we're reaching less than 2% of U.S. eye care practices."**
   There are ~62,000 optometry + ophthalmology practices in the U.S. Enormous expansion runway.

5. **"Insurance reimbursement in Year 4 removes the $250 out-of-pocket barrier."**
   Self-pay is the bottleneck in Y1-Y2; removing it accelerates adoption.

6. **"Our SAM filtering is conservative — we excluded primary care, DTC, employer wellness, and under-35 family-driven demand entirely."**
   Every excluded segment is upside, not risk.

7. **"PLA code in 6-9 months gives us a unique billing code before FDA clearance."**
   Following the exact playbook of Exact Sciences and BillionToOne.
